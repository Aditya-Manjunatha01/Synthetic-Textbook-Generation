[SUMMARY: 3.2.3 — Exercise: Deriving the Clapeyron Equation for the Solid-Liquid Transition of Water and the Resulting Slope of the Melting Curve]

ESTABLISHED:
  — Derived the specific form of the Clapeyron equation for the solid-liquid transition of water.
  — Demonstrated that the slope $dP/dT$ is negative for water by analyzing the signs of the latent heat of fusion ($L_{\text{fus}} > 0$), absolute temperature ($T_m > 0$), and the change in molar volume ($\Delta v_{\text{fus}} < 0$).
  — Established the relationship $\Delta v_{\text{fus}} = M_{\text{H}_2\text{O}} (1/\rho_{\text{water}} - 1/\rho_{\text{ice}})$, proving that because ice is less dense than liquid water, the volume decreases upon melting.
  — Concluded that increasing pressure favors the denser liquid phase, thereby decreasing the equilibrium melting temperature.

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  $L_{\text{fus}}$ : molar latent heat of fusion [J mol$^{-1}$]
  $\Delta v_{\text{fus}}$ : change in molar volume during fusion [m$^3$ mol$^{-1}$]
  $T_m$ : melting temperature [K]
  $P_m$ : melting pressure [Pa]
  $v_{\text{water}}$ : molar volume of liquid water [m$^3$ mol$^{-1}$]
  $v_{\text{ice}}$ : molar volume of ice [m$^3$ mol$^{-1}$]
  $M_{\text{H}_2\text{O}}$ : molar mass of water [kg mol$^{-1}$]
  $\rho_{\text{water}}$ : mass density of liquid water [kg m$^{-3}$]
  $\rho_{\text{ice}}$ : mass density of ice [kg m$^{-3}$]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  — Guided derivation: The subsection leads the student through a step-by-step application of a previously established general law (Clapeyron) to a specific physical case.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  $\left( \frac{dP}{dT} \right)_{\text{fus}} = \frac{L_{\text{fus}}}{T_m \Delta v_{\text{fus}}}$
  $\Delta v_{\text{fus}} = M_{\text{H}_2\text{O}} \left( \frac{1}{\rho_{\text{water}}} - \frac{1}{\rho_{\text{ice}}} \right)$
