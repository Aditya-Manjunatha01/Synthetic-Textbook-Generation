[SUMMARY: 1.3.1 — Internal Energy and Heat Capacities of Ideal Gases]

ESTABLISHED:
  — Internal energy of an ideal gas depends solely on temperature, $U = U(T)$, derived from the Joule expansion thought experiment where $\delta Q = 0$ and $\delta W = 0$, implying $(\partial U/\partial V)_T = 0$.
  — Defined molar heat capacity at constant volume $C_{V,m}$ as the temperature derivative of molar internal energy.
  — Defined molar heat capacity at constant pressure $C_{P,m}$ based on heat added during isobaric processes.
  — Derived Mayer's Relation by applying the First Law to an isobaric process and substituting the ideal gas law $PV = nRT$ to account for expansion work.

NOT YET DEVELOPED:
  — Specific values of $C_{V,m}$ and $C_{P,m}$ for different molecular geometries (monatomic, diatomic, polyatomic).
  — The temperature dependence of heat capacities (e.g., rotational freezing).

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  — $U$ : internal energy [J]
  — $U_m$ : molar internal energy [J/mol]
  — $C_{V,m}$ : molar heat capacity at constant volume [J/(mol·K)]
  — $C_{P,m}$ : molar heat capacity at constant pressure [J/(mol·K)]
  — $R$ : universal gas constant [J/(mol·K)]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  — Full derivation provided for Joule's Law and Mayer's Relation.
  — Physical intuition provided via the Joule expansion thought experiment and the distinction between heating at constant volume vs. constant pressure.
  — No worked examples or reader exercises included in this subsection.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  — $(\partial U/\partial V)_T = 0$
  — $C_{V,m} = \frac{1}{n} \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial T} \right)_V$
  — $dU = n C_{V,m} dT$
  — $C_{P,m} - C_{V,m} = R$
