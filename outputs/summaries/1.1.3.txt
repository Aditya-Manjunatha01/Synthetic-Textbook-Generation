[SUMMARY: 1.1.3 — Equations of State from Ideal Gas to Van der Waals and Virial Expansions]

ESTABLISHED
  — The Ideal Gas Law as a baseline for non-interacting point particles.
  — The Van der Waals Equation of State, derived by applying two corrections to the ideal gas law: the excluded volume correction $(V_m - b)$ and the cohesion pressure correction $(P + a/V_m^2)$.
  — The Compressibility Factor $Z$, used to determine if attractive forces ($Z < 1$) or repulsive/volume effects ($Z > 1$) dominate.
  — The Virial Expansion as a power series in density ($1/V_m$), where coefficients $B(T)$ and $C(T)$ correspond to pair and triplet interactions respectively.

NOT YET DEVELOPED
  None.

NOTATION INTRODUCED
  $P$ : pressure [Pa]
  $V_m$ : molar volume [$\text{m}^3/\text{mol}$]
  $T$ : temperature [K]
  $R$ : universal gas constant [$\text{J/mol}\cdot\text{K}$]
  $a$ : Van der Waals attraction parameter [$\text{Pa}\cdot\text{m}^6/\text{mol}^2$]
  $b$ : Van der Waals excluded volume parameter [$\text{m}^3/\text{mol}$]
  $Z$ : compressibility factor [dimensionless]
  $B(T)$ : second virial coefficient [$\text{m}^3/\text{mol}$]
  $C(T)$ : third virial coefficient [$\text{m}^6/\text{mol}^2$]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH
  — Heuristic derivation for the Van der Waals equation based on physical intuition (molecular size and attraction).
  — Mathematical definition of the compressibility factor and Virial series.
  — Included a worked example calculating $Z$ using the Van der Waals equation.
  — Physical interpretation of $Z$ provided.

KEY EQUATIONS
  — Ideal Gas Law: $PV_m = RT$
  — Van der Waals Equation: $\left( P + \frac{a}{V_m^2} \right)(V_m - b) = RT$
  — Compressibility Factor: $Z = \frac{PV_m}{RT}$
  — Virial Expansion: $Z = 1 + \frac{B(T)}{V_m} + \frac{C(T)}{V_m^2} + \dots$
