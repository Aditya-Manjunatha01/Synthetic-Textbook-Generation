[SUMMARY: 2.3.3 — Exercise: Deriving the Gibbs-Helmholtz Equation and its Application to Temperature Dependence of Equilibrium Constants]

ESTABLISHED:
  - Derivation of the Gibbs-Helmholtz equation: Starting from $G = H - TS$ and $S = -(\partial G/\partial T)_P$, the temperature dependence of the ratio $G/T$ was derived using the quotient rule.
  - The Gibbs-Helmholtz relation for a process: $(\partial(\Delta G/T)/\partial T)_P = -\Delta H/T^2$.
  - Derivation of the van 't Hoff equation: By substituting $\Delta G^\circ = -RT \ln K$ into the Gibbs-Helmholtz equation, the relationship between the equilibrium constant $K$ and temperature $T$ was established.
  - Integration of the van 't Hoff equation: For a constant $\Delta H^\circ$, the relationship between equilibrium constants at two different temperatures is $\ln(K_2/K_1) = (\Delta H^\circ/R)(1/T_1 - 1/T_2)$.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  - $\left( \frac{\partial(G/T)}{\partial T} \right)_P = -\frac{H}{T^2}$
  - $\left( \frac{\partial(\Delta G/T)}{\partial T} \right)_P = -\frac{\Delta H}{T^2}$
  - $\frac{d \ln K}{dT} = \frac{\Delta H^\circ}{RT^2}$
  - $\ln \left( \frac{K_2}{K_1} \right) = \frac{\Delta H^\circ}{R} \left( \frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{1}{T_2} \right)$
