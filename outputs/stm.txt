The requirement for finite entropy as $T \to 0$ has been established, proving that the molar heat capacity $C_P$ must vanish as $T \to 0$ such that $C_P \propto T^n$ with $n > 0$. This ensures the convergence of the integral $S(T) = \int_0^T (C_P/T') dT'$. Standard models (Debye, electronic, Einstein) are confirmed to satisfy this condition. The notation $C_0$ and $n$ were used for the general power-law model. No new approximations are in effect beyond the power-law assumption for the exercise.
