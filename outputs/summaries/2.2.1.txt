[SUMMARY: 2.2.1 — Construction of Thermodynamic Potentials and the Legendre Transform]

ESTABLISHED:
  — The fundamental thermodynamic relation is derived by combining the First Law ($dU = \delta Q - \delta W$) with reversible definitions $\delta Q = T dS$ and $\delta W = P dV$, yielding $dU = T dS - P dV$.
  — The Legendre transform is defined as $h(g) = f(x) - gx$, allowing a change of independent variables from $x$ to the conjugate variable $g = df/dx$.
  — Three potentials are constructed via Legendre transforms of $U(S, V)$:
    1. Enthalpy: $H = U + PV$ with $dH = T dS + V dP$.
    2. Helmholtz Free Energy: $F = U - TS$ with $dF = -S dT - P dV$.
    3. Gibbs Free Energy: $G = H - TS$ (or $F + PV$) with $dG = -S dT + V dP$.
  — Demonstrated via an ideal gas example that the equation of state $P = RT/V$ is recovered by differentiating the appropriate potential ($F$) with respect to its natural variable.

NOT YET DEVELOPED:
  — The derivation of Maxwell relations from the exactness of these differentials.

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  — $U$ : internal energy [J]
  — $S$ : entropy [J/K]
  — $V$ : volume [m$^3$]
  — $T$ : temperature [K]
  — $P$ : pressure [Pa]
  — $H$ : enthalpy [J]
  — $F$ : Helmholtz free energy [J]
  — $G$ : Gibbs free energy [J]
  — $\mathcal{L}$ : Legendre transform operator [dimensionless]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  — Full mathematical derivation of potentials.
  — Physical intuition provided regarding "natural" vs. "experimental" variables.
  — Included a worked example using an ideal gas to demonstrate practical utility.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  — $dU = T dS - P dV$
  — $H = U + PV \implies dH = T dS + V dP$
  — $F = U - TS \implies dF = -S dT - P dV$
  — $G = H - TS \implies dG = -S dT + V dP$
