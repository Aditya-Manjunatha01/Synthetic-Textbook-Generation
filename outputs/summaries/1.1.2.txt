[SUMMARY: 1.1.2 — Worked Example: Determining Degrees of Freedom and State Variable Independence]

ESTABLISHED:
  - Defined degrees of freedom $\text{f}$ as the minimum number of independent intensive properties required to uniquely specify a system's state.
  - Derived the formula $\text{f} = \text{N} - \text{R}$, where $\text{N}$ is the number of intensive variables and $\text{R}$ is the number of independent constraints.
  - Demonstrated for a single-phase pure substance that $\text{N}=3$ (using $P, T, v$) and $\text{R}=1$ (the equation of state), resulting in $\text{f}=2$.
  - Established that the existence of a functional relationship $f(P, V, T) = 0$ renders a set of variables dependent and reduces the dimensionality of the state space to a surface.

NOT YET DEVELOPED:
  - Specific derivations of the Van der Waals and Virial equations of state.
  - The role of entropy $s$ in state space projections (mentioned as $T$-$s$ diagrams but not derived).

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  - $\text{f}$ : degrees of freedom [dimensionless]
  - $\text{N}$ : number of state variables [dimensionless]
  - $\text{R}$ : number of independent constraints [dimensionless]
  - $v$ : specific volume [$\text{m}^3/\text{kg}$]
  - $m$ : mass [$\text{kg}$]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  - Used a worked example of a piston-cylinder system to apply theoretical concepts.
  - Provided a systematic counting procedure for degrees of freedom.
  - Linked the mathematical concept of functional dependence to the geometric interpretation of state space surfaces and 2D projections.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  - $v = \frac{V}{m}$
  - $\text{f} = \text{N} - \text{R}$
  - $PV - nRT = 0$
