

<<CONCEPT: Nernst Heat Theorem>>
name        : Nernst Heat Theorem
status      : introduced
location    : 3.3.1
depends_on  : []
statement   : For any isothermal process involving condensed phases, the change in entropy vanishes as the temperature approaches absolute zero.
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Third Law of Thermodynamics>>
name        : Third Law of Thermodynamics
status      : introduced
location    : 3.3.1
depends_on  : []
statement   : As the temperature of a perfect crystalline substance approaches absolute zero, its entropy approaches a universal constant (conventionally zero).
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Vanishing Heat Capacity>>
name        : Vanishing Heat Capacity
status      : proven
location    : 3.3.1
depends_on  : [Third Law of Thermodynamics]
statement   : For the entropy to remain finite as T approaches zero, the heat capacities C_P and C_V must vanish as T approaches absolute zero.
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Absolute Entropy Determination>>
name        : Absolute Entropy Determination
status      : proven
location    : 3.3.2
depends_on  : []
statement   : The absolute entropy of a substance is determined by integrating the heat capacity divided by temperature from absolute zero to T, using a combination of the Debye $T^3$ law for low-temperature extrapolation and experimental data.
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Debye T^3 Law>>
name        : Debye T^3 Law
status      : introduced
location    : 3.3.2
depends_on  : []
statement   : For non-metallic crystalline solids at very low temperatures, the heat capacity is proportional to the cube of the absolute temperature.
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Entropy Convergence Condition>>
name        : Entropy Convergence Condition
status      : proven
location    : 3.3.3
depends_on  : []
statement   : For absolute entropy to remain finite as T approaches 0, the heat capacity C_P must vanish as T to the power of n where n > 0.
<</CONCEPT>>
