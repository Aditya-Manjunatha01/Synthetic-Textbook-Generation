[SUMMARY: 2.1.2 — Worked Example: Entropy Change in an Irreversible Free Expansion of an Ideal Gas]

ESTABLISHED:
  — The entropy change for the free expansion (Joule expansion) of an ideal gas into a vacuum.
  — Proof that for an adiabatic free expansion of an ideal gas, $\Delta U = 0$ and $T$ remains constant because $U = U(T)$.
  — The method of using a hypothetical reversible isothermal path to calculate $\Delta S$ for an irreversible process, leveraging the fact that entropy is a state function.
  — Final result: $\Delta S = nR \ln(V_f/V_i)$.

  $V_f$ : final volume [m$^3$]
  $T$ : absolute temperature [K]

KEY EQUATIONS:
  $\Delta U = \delta Q - \delta W = 0$
  $\delta Q_{rev} = \frac{nRT}{V} dV$
  $\Delta S = nR \ln\left(\frac{V_f}{V_i}\right)$
