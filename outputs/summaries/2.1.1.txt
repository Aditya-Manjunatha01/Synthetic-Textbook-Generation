[SUMMARY: 2.1.1 — Thermodynamic Definition of Entropy and the Clausius Inequality]

ESTABLISHED:
  - Definition of entropy $S$ as a state function for reversible processes based on the Carnot cycle's efficiency.
  - Proof that for a reversible cycle, the cyclic integral of $\delta Q_{rev}/T$ is zero, making $dS$ an exact differential.
  - The Clausius Inequality for any cycle: $\oint \delta Q/T \le 0$.
  - The general relation for any process: $dS \ge \delta Q/T$, where equality holds for reversible processes.
  - The principle that for adiabatic processes ($\delta Q = 0$), $dS \ge 0$.

NOT YET DEVELOPED:

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  - $S$ : entropy [J/K]
  - $\delta Q_{rev}$ : infinitesimal heat exchanged in a reversible process [J]
  - $\delta Q$ : infinitesimal heat exchanged in any process [J]
  - $T$ : absolute temperature [K]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  - Full derivation of the state function property from the Carnot cycle.
  - Heuristic and logical transition from reversible to irreversible paths.

  - No worked examples or exercises included in this subsection.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  - $dS = \frac{\delta Q_{rev}}{T}$
  - $\oint \frac{\delta Q_{rev}}{T} = 0$
  - $\Delta S = \int_1^2 \frac{\delta Q_{rev}}{T}$
  - $\oint \frac{\delta Q}{T} \leq 0$
  - $dS \geq \frac{\delta Q}{T}$
