

<<CONCEPT: Chemical Potential>>
name: Chemical Potential
status: introduced
location: 3.1.1
depends_on: []
statement: The change in internal energy or Gibbs free energy of a system when an infinitesimal amount of a species is added, acting as the driving force for mass transport.
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Diffusive Equilibrium>>
name: Diffusive Equilibrium
status: introduced
location: 3.1.1
depends_on: [Chemical Potential]
statement: A state in which the chemical potential of a species is uniform across all regions or phases of a system, resulting in no net flux of matter.
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Thermodynamic Equilibrium>>
name: Thermodynamic Equilibrium
status: introduced
location: 3.1.3
depends_on: []
statement: The state of a system characterized by the absence of macroscopic driving forces, requiring simultaneous thermal, mechanical, and chemical equilibrium.
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Thermal Equilibrium>>
name: Thermal Equilibrium
status: proven
location: 3.1.3
depends_on: []
statement: The condition where temperature is uniform across all phases of a system (T_\alpha = T_\beta).
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Mechanical Equilibrium>>
name: Mechanical Equilibrium
status: proven
location: 3.1.3
depends_on: []
statement: The condition where pressure is equal in every phase of a system (P_\alpha = P_\beta).
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Chemical Equilibrium>>
name: Chemical Equilibrium
status: proven
location: 3.1.3
depends_on: []
statement: The condition where the chemical potential of each species is identical in every phase it inhabits (\mu_{i,\alpha} = \mu_{i,\beta}).
<</CONCEPT>>

<<CONCEPT: Raoult's Law>>
name: Raoult's Law
status: proven
location: 3.1.3
depends_on: []
statement: The partial pressure of a component in an ideal mixture is equal to the product of its mole fraction in the liquid and its pure component vapor pressure (x_A P_A^* = y_A P).
<</CONCEPT>>
