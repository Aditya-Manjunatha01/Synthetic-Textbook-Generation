[SUMMARY: 3.3.3 — Exercise: Analyzing the Behavior of Heat Capacities as T approaches Zero to Ensure Finite Entropy]

ESTABLISHED:
  — Derived the condition for the convergence of the absolute entropy integral $S(T) = \int_0^T \frac{C_P(T')}{T'} dT'$ as $T \to 0$.
  — Analyzed a power-law model $C_P(T) = C_0 T^n$ and determined that the integral converges if and only if $n > 0$.
  — Demonstrated that for $n=0$, the entropy diverges logarithmically, and for $n < 0$, it diverges by power law.
  — Verified that the Debye model ($n=3$), electronic heat capacity ($n=1$), and Einstein model (exponential decay) are consistent with the Third Law of Thermodynamics.

  — $n$ : dimensionless exponent of temperature dependence [dimensionless]
