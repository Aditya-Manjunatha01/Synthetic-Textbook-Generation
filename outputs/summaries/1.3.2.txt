[SUMMARY: 1.3.2 — Worked Example: Calculating Molar Heat Capacities for a Diatomic Gas with Rotational Freezing]

ESTABLISHED:
  — The concept of "frozen" degrees of freedom: rotational energy levels are discrete, and modes only contribute to heat capacity when $k_B T \approx \Delta E_{rot}$.
  — Defined the characteristic rotational temperature $\Theta_{rot}$ based on the moment of inertia $I$.
  — Derived $C_{V,m}$ for a diatomic gas using a simplified rotational energy model $U_m^{rot} \approx RT [ 1 - \frac{1}{2} (\frac{\Theta_{rot}}{T})^2 ]$.
  — Established the temperature limits for $C_{V,m}$ and $C_{P,m}$ for $\text{N}_2$:
    - Low $T$ ($T \ll \Theta_{rot}$): $C_{V,m} \approx \frac{3}{2}R$, $C_{P,m} \approx \frac{5}{2}R$.
    - High $T$ ($T \gg \Theta_{rot}$): $C_{V,m} \approx \frac{5}{2}R$, $C_{P,m} \approx \frac{7}{2}R$.

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  — $\Theta_{rot}$ : characteristic rotational temperature [K]
  — $I$ : moment of inertia [kg·m²]
  — $U_m^{trans}$ : molar translational internal energy [J/mol]
  — $U_m^{rot}$ : molar rotational internal energy [J/mol]
  — $C_{V,m}^{rot}$ : rotational contribution to molar heat capacity at constant volume [J/(mol·K)]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  — Worked example using Nitrogen ($\text{N}_2$) to illustrate theoretical concepts.
  — Combination of classical equipartition theory and quantum mechanical corrections.
  — Physical intuition provided via the description of "step" behavior in heat capacity plots.
  — Summary table used to consolidate results across temperature regimes.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  — $\Theta_{rot} = \frac{\hbar^2}{2Ik_B}$
  — $U_m^{rot}(T) \approx RT \left[ 1 - \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\Theta_{rot}}{T} \right)^2 \right]$
  — $C_{V,m} = \frac{3}{2}R + R \left( 1 + \frac{\Theta_{rot}^2}{2T^2} \right)$
  — $C_{P,m} = C_{V,m} + R$
