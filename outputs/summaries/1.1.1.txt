[SUMMARY: 1.1.1 — Classification of Thermodynamic Systems and State Variables]

ESTABLISHED:
  - Definitions of system, surroundings, and boundary.
  - Classification of systems based on boundary permeability:
    - Isolated: $\Delta m = 0, \Delta E = 0$.
    - Closed: $\Delta m = 0, \Delta E \neq 0$.
    - Open: $\Delta m \neq 0, \Delta E \neq 0$.
  - Definition of state variables as path-independent properties.
  - Distinction between extensive variables (scale with system size) and intensive variables (independent of system size).
  - Procedure for creating intensive properties from extensive ones by dividing by an additive extensive variable (e.g., $V/n$).

NOT YET DEVELOPED:
  - Criteria for determining the minimum number of independent state variables required to define a state (Degrees of Freedom).

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  - $m$ : mass [kg]
  - $n$ : number of moles [mol]
  - $U$ : internal energy [J]
  - $X_i$ : general state variable [dimensionless/variable]
  - $T$ : temperature [K]
  - $P$ : pressure [Pa]
  - $V$ : volume [m$^3$]
  - $\lambda$ : scaling factor [dimensionless]
  - $X_{ext}$ : extensive variable [variable]
  - $X_{int}$ : intensive variable [variable]
  - $V_m$ : molar volume [m$^3$/mol]
  - $u$ : specific internal energy [J/kg]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  - Conceptual definitions followed by mathematical scaling rules.
  - Physical intuition provided via system classification and scaling examples.
  - Visual aids referenced (Figures 1 and 2) to support classification and scaling concepts.
  - No worked examples or exercises included in this subsection.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  - $X_{ext} \rightarrow \lambda X_{ext}$
  - $X_{int} \rightarrow X_{int}$
  - $V_m = V/n$
  - $u = U/m$
