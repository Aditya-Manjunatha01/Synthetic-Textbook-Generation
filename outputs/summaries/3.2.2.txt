[SUMMARY: 3.2.2 — Worked Example: Calculating the Boiling Point of Water at High Pressure using the Clausius-Clapeyron Relation]

ESTABLISHED:
  — Derived the integrated form of the Clausius-Clapeyron equation by separating variables and integrating $\int dP/P = \int (L/RT^2) dT$.
  — Established the final integrated expression: $\ln(P_2/P_1) = \frac{L}{R} \left( \frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{1}{T_2} \right)$.
  — Demonstrated application via a worked example for water at $P_2 = 10\text{ atm}$, yielding $T_2 \approx 452.67\text{ K}$.
  — Identified two primary assumptions: constant molar latent heat $L$ over the temperature range and ideal gas behavior of the vapor.

NOT YET DEVELOPED:
  — Corrections for non-ideal gas behavior (compressibility factors).
  — Temperature dependence of the latent heat $L$ near the critical point.

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  $P_1$ : Initial pressure [Pa or atm]
  $T_1$ : Initial boiling temperature [K]
  $P_2$ : Final pressure [Pa or atm]
  $T_2$ : Final boiling temperature [K]
  $L$ : Molar latent heat of vaporization [J/mol]
  $R$ : Universal gas constant [J$\cdot$mol$^{-1}\cdot$K$^{-1}$]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  — Mathematical rigour: Full derivation of the integrated form from the differential form.
  — Physical intuition: Context provided via pressure cookers/autoclaves and a description of the $P-T$ phase diagram.
  — Worked examples: Included a detailed step-by-step calculation for water at 10 atm.
  — Exercises: None.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  $\frac{dP}{dT} = \frac{PL}{RT^2}$
  $\ln\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right) = \frac{L}{R} \left( \frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{1}{T_2} \right)$
  $T_2 = \left[ \frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{R}{L} \ln\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right) \right]^{-1}$
