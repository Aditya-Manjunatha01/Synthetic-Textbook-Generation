[SUMMARY: 2.2.2 — Derivation of the Maxwell Relations from Exact Differentials]

ESTABLISHED:
  — The derivation of the four Maxwell relations based on the property that thermodynamic potentials are state functions with exact differentials.
  — The application of Schwarz's theorem (equality of mixed partial derivatives) to the fundamental relations of $U, H, F,$ and $G$.
  — The identification of natural variables for each potential: $U(S, V)$, $H(S, P)$, $F(T, V)$, and $G(T, P)$.

NOT YET DEVELOPED:
  — Practical application of these relations to derive other thermodynamic identities (e.g., internal pressure).

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  — $U$ : Internal Energy [J]
  — $H$ : Enthalpy [J]
  — $F$ : Helmholtz Free Energy [J]
  — $G$ : Gibbs Free Energy [J]
  — $S$ : Entropy [J/K]
  — $V$ : Volume [m$^3$]
  — $T$ : Temperature [K]
  — $P$ : Pressure [Pa]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  — Full mathematical derivation using multivariable calculus.
  — Physical intuition provided via the concept of state functions and the "Thermodynamic Square" mnemonic.
  — No worked examples or exercises included in this subsection.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  — $\left( \frac{\partial T}{\partial V} \right)_S = -\left( \frac{\partial P}{\partial S} \right)_V$
  — $\left( \frac{\partial T}{\partial P} \right)_S = \left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} \right)_P$
  — $\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial V} \right)_T = \left( \frac{\partial P}{\partial T} \right)_V$
  — $-\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial P} \right)_T = \left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial T} \right)_P$
