[SUMMARY: 3.3.2 — Worked Example: Determining the Absolute Entropy of a Crystal using Low-Temperature Heat Capacity Integration]

ESTABLISHED:
  — The method for calculating absolute entropy $S(T)$ using the Third Law boundary condition $S(0) = 0$.
  — The integration strategy for absolute entropy: splitting the integral into a theoretical extrapolation from $0$ to $T_{low}$ and a numerical integration from $T_{low}$ to $T$.
  — The use of the Debye $T^3$ law ($C_P \approx \beta T^3$) to resolve the experimental gap at very low temperatures.
  — A worked example demonstrating the summation of the analytical Debye contribution and the trapezoidal numerical integration of experimental $C_P/T$ data.

NOT YET DEVELOPED:
  — Formal proof or analysis of the convergence of the integral $\int (C_P/T) dT$ as $T \to 0$.

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  — $S(T)$ : absolute entropy at temperature $T$ [J/(mol$\cdot$K)]
  — $S_0$ : entropy at absolute zero [J/(mol$\cdot$K)]
  — $T_{low}$ : minimum temperature of reliable experimental measurement [K]
  — $\beta$ : material-specific low-temperature heat capacity coefficient [J/(mol$\cdot$K$^4$)]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  — Full derivation of the integration formula from the thermodynamic identity.
  — Physical intuition provided regarding the "experimental gap" and the use of the Debye approximation.
  — A detailed worked example including a data table and step-by-step numerical calculation using the trapezoidal rule.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  — $S(T) = \int_{0}^{T} \frac{C_P(T')}{T'} dT'$
  — $C_P \approx \beta T^3$
  — $S_{low} = \frac{1}{3}\beta T_{low}^3$
