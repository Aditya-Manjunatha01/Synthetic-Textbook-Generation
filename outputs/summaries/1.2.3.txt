[SUMMARY: 1.2.3 — Exercise: Deriving the First Law for a System with Variable Mass Flow]

ESTABLISHED:
  — Mass balance for a control volume (CV) based on the conservation of mass.
  — Concept of flow work: the work required to push a differential mass $dm$ into a system against pressure $P$, defined as $\delta W_{flow} = P v \, dm$.
  — Definition of enthalpy ($H$) and specific enthalpy ($h$) as the sum of internal energy and flow work.
  — General energy balance for an open system (First Law for a CV) expressed as a rate of change of system energy.
  — Application to a steady-state, adiabatic nozzle, proving that the decrease in enthalpy equals the increase in kinetic energy.

NOT YET DEVELOPED:
  — Specific relations for enthalpy and internal energy for ideal gases.
  — Molar heat capacities.

NOTATION INTRODUCED:
  — $\dot{m}_{in}, \dot{m}_{out}$ : mass flow rates [kg/s]
  — $v$ : specific volume [m³/kg]
  — $\delta W_{flow}$ : differential flow work [J]
  — $H$ : enthalpy [J]
  — $h$ : specific enthalpy [J/kg]
  — $\dot{Q}$ : rate of heat transfer [W]
  — $\dot{W}$ : rate of work done by the system [W]
  — $e$ : total specific energy [J/kg]

PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH:
  — Full derivation of the open-system energy balance.
  — Physical intuition provided for flow work and the role of enthalpy.
  — A worked example (steady-state nozzle) was included to apply the general formula.
  — No reader exercises were included.

KEY EQUATIONS:
  — $\frac{dm}{dt} = \sum \dot{m}_{in} - \sum \dot{m}_{out}$
  — $h = u + Pv$
  — $\frac{dU_{sys}}{dt} = \dot{Q} - \dot{W} + \sum \dot{m}_{in} \left( h + \frac{1}{2}v^2 + gz \right)_{in} - \sum \dot{m}_{out} \left( h + \frac{1}{2}v^2 + gz \right)_{out}$
  — $h_{in} - h_{out} = \frac{1}{2}(v_{out}^2 - v_{in}^2)$
